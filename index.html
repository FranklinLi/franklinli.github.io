About

Research with Impact

Empowering Independence Through Technology

My research sits at the intersection of HCI, Assistive Technology, and Ubiquitous Computing. I design and deploy systems that help people with disabilities navigate daily activities with greater independence and dignity.

My dissertation focuses on non-visual cooking for people with vision impairments, developing AI-powered systems that enable hands-free, eyes-free access to kitchen tasks. Before CMU, I completed my M.Sc. at University of Toronto with Prof. Khai Truong.

I'm committed to building technology that serves everyone, with a particular focus on marginalized communities and real-world impact.
