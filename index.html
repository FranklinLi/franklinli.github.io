About Me

Research that Matters

I am a final-year PhD candidate at Carnegie Mellon University's Human-Computer Interaction Institute, advised by Prof. Patrick A. Carrington. My research sits at the intersection of HCI, Assistive Technology, and Ubiquitous Computing.

My dissertation focuses on enabling non-visual cooking for people with vision impairments through AI and assistive technologies. I've developed systems that help users understand their surroundings, track progress in complex tasks, and navigate daily activities with greater independence.

Before CMU, I completed my M.Sc. at the University of Toronto under Prof. Khai N. Truong, following my B.Eng. in Electrical Engineering.
